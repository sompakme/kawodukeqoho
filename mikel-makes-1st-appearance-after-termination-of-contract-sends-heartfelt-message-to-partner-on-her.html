Reports recently linked Mikel to be in contact with Tottenham boss Antonio Conte for a possible January switch to north London.

Mikel's career in brief

The ex-Super Eagles was part of the Nigerian team coached by the late Stepehn Keshi that won AFCON 2013 in South Africa.

He was twice an Olympic medalist with Nigeria at the 200 Beijing Games where he won silver and the Rio 2016 edition, taking home bronze medal in the football event.

The former Chelsea star is still regarded as Nigerian most successful player where he won the Champions League and the Europa League titles with the Stamford Bridge club.

He was also part of the Blues squad that lifted the Premier League title twice and three FA Cup trophies during his 11 spell at west London.

Mikel quits club in Kuwait

Meanwhile, Legit.ng had earlier reported that Kuwait Sporting Club have announced that their contract with former Nigerian international, Mikel Obi has been terminated.

The club, via their official media account, made the disclosure on Thursday that they have parted ways with the former Super Eagles captain who joined them about four months ago.

On July 1 this year, the 34-year-old cancelled his contract agreement with English club Stoke City and moved to Kuwait SC.

Conte reportedly contacts Mikel, Moses for possible move to Tottenham in January

Legit.ng had also reported that Antonio Conte has reportedly got in touch with former Super Eagles captain, John Obi Mikel and Victor Moses, Team Nigeria UK, Brila.

The former Nigerian internationals had played under the Italian manager during his spell at Stamford Bridge.

In fact, Mikel had to end his 11-year spell with the West London club after Conte dimmed him surplus to requirements.
