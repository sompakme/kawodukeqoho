Former American government employee Dana Hyde is presently the CEO of the Millennium Challenge Company (MCC).

The MCC is a separate U.S. government organization that offers development aid to support economic development and poverty eradication in low-income nations.

Hyde had a number of government jobs prior to her appointment at the MCC, including Associate Director of the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) and Assistant to the Administrator for Management at the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID).

She had experience working for many organizations as a consultant and in the private sector.

Hyde graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in English from Williams College and a Master of Public Policy from the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University.
