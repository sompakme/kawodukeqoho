For dissolved air, you can remove it by raising the temperature of the fluid until the air is released. This should only be done if absolutely necessary as hydraulic oil will normally tend to be at least 10% dissolved air. What will happen if there is air trapped in the hydraulic system? When air contaminates a hydraulic fluid, usually via the pump's inlet, aeration, cavitation, or foaming can occur.
