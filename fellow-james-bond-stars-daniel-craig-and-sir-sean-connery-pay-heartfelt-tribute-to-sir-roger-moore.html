Fellow James Bond stars Daniel Craig and Sir Sean Connery pay heartfelt tribute to Sir Roger Moore

 Posted on August 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  501 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Pierce Brosnan, Daniel Craig and Sean Connery all paid their respects

    Sir Sean Connery, the first actor to play , has paid an emotional tribute to Sir Roger Moore, who passed away on Monday. Sir Roger - who starred in seven Bond films, including  and - died in Switzerland, aged 89, after a "short but brave" battle with cancer. Sir Sean, 86, credited his successor and revealed their long friendship was "filled with jokes and laughter". The Scottish actor said in a statement: "I was very sad to hear of Roger's passing, we had an unusually long relationship by Hollywood standards, that was filled with jokes and laughter. I will miss him."

    Referencing the theme song of Sir Roger's , current 007 star Daniel Craig wrote on the official James Bond Twitter page: "Nobody Does It Better - love Daniel." Pierce Brosnan, who has starred in four Bond movies, described the late actor as "magnificent" alongside a picture of the pair of them together. "Dear Sir Roger Moore, it is indeed with a heavy heart that I hear the news of your passing this morning," he said. "You were a big part of my life, from The Saint to James Bond... you were a magnificent James Bond and one that led the way for me. The world will miss you and your unique sense of humour for years to come. My sincerest condolences to your family and children. RIP."
