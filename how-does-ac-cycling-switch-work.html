How does AC cycling switch work?

The AC clutch cycling switch is a critical component of a contemporary vehicle’s air conditioning system’s operation. When installed on the low pressure side of the air conditioning system, its function is to detect the flow of refrigerant through the system by measuring the pressure. When the pressure returns to normal, the cycling switch will be activated and will be closed.

 

Another thing to be aware of is what a clutch cycling pressure switch is.

The clutch cycling switch is a transducer, which is a kind of electronic device that changes internal resistance in reaction to changes in pressure or temperature. The clutch cycling switch, which is typically positioned on the accumulator, senses the low side A/C pressure near the evaporator outlet and cycles the clutch.
