Maxwell Clark, also known as Max Clark, is an American baseball outfielder and pitcher committed to playing college baseball at Vanderbilt University, Nashville, Tennessee.

Born on December 21, 2004, in Bridgend, Wales, Max attended Franklin Community High School in Franklin, Indiana, where he played as an outfielder and pitcher on their team. The talented Max is already a potential No. 1 overall pick in the 2023 Major League Baseball draft after a hugely successful high school career. He most recently culminated his senior year batting .646 with six home runs, 33 RBIs, and 45 runs scored, while also being named the Gatorade Baseball Player of the Year.

Max has also represented Team USA at the 2021 WBSC U-18 World Cup, helping lead them to a gold medal with a .294 batting average over eight games.

Max is the best prospect to come out of Indiana since Bryan Bullington went No. 1 overall in 2002. He is a viable candidate for the Pirates with the top pick, it will be interesting to see where he is headed. His sweet left-handed swing and a mature approach focusing on smoking line drives from gap to gap are sure to find multiple suitors in the upcoming draft.

With a batting average of 577, nine home runs, 32 RBIs, 31 walks, and 22 stolen bases in his junior year on the baseball team, he won the Indiana Gatorade Baseball Player of the Year award for the second consecutive year. He played in the USA Baseball National Training Complex's Prospect Development Pipeline League in Cary, North Carolina, that summer. In addition, he participated in the Perfect Game All-American Classic at Chase Field and hit 294 throughout eight games to help lead Team USA to a gold medal in the 2021 WBSC U-18 World Cup. As the top high school draft prospect for the 2018 MLB draft, Clark went into his final year of high school.

The Detroit Tigers chose Clark as the third overall choice in the first round of the 2023 Major League Baseball draft. Clark signed a $7 million contract with the Tigers on July 17, 2023. After making his professional debut, Clark played for both the Lakeland Flying Tigers and the Florida Complex League Tigers throughout the season. Between the two teams, he hit 224/383/ 376 across 23 games, driving in 19 runs and two home runs.
